lit review
3D human pose estimation in video with temporal convolutions and semi-supervised training (videopose3d, cvpr 2019)
uses temporal conv nets to lift 2d keypoints into 3d, work swell on video sequences, doesn’t need full 3d labels (semi-supervised), gives smooth stable 3d poses from 2d input.
- https://openaccess.thecvf.com/content_CVPR_2019/papers/Pavllo_3D_Human_Pose_Estimation_in_Video_With_Temporal_Convolutions_and_CVPR_2019_paper.pdf

gardner et al. (deep parametric indoor lighting estimation, iccv 2019)
predicts lighting setup (direction + intensity) from a single indoor image, represents lights parametrically, shows deep nets can infer lighting from shadows and reflections.
- https://openaccess.thecvf.com/content_ICCV_2019/papers/Gardner_Deep_Parametric_Indoor_Lighting_Estimation_ICCV_2019_paper.pdf

light direction and color estimation, 2020)
regression model predicting light direction and color from one image, smaller and more practical architecture, works on both synthetic and real data, good baseline for lighting extraction.
- https://arxiv.org/abs/2009.08941

deep 3d human pose estimation: a review, 2021)
comprehensive survey on 3d pose estimation methods, covers 2d detectors, 3d lifters, temporal models, and datasets, highlights challenges like occlusion and depth ambiguity.
- https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1077314221000692

editable indoor lighting estimation, eccv 2022
moder napproach combining neural and parametric lighting, outputs editable lighting parameters for interpretability, bridges rendering and deep learning.
- https://arxiv.org/abs/2211.03928
